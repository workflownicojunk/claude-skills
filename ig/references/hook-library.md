## Category 1: Correction Format (PROVEN #1)

**Pattern:** "Die meisten Frauen [verb] [topic] voellig falsch."
**Why it works:** Triple mechanic: curiosity gap ("what am I doing wrong?") + identity trigger ("this is about ME") + promise of expert correction. The word "falsch" creates cognitive dissonance that demands resolution.
**Performance:** Avg 1,876 likes, max 14,515. Save rates consistently 4.5-5.3%.

## Category 3: Bold Claim / Surprising Statement

**Pattern:** Opens with a statement that feels counterintuitive or provocative.
**Why it works:** Creates cognitive dissonance. The viewer's existing belief is challenged, and they must watch to resolve the tension. Works especially well when backed by the creator's authority and relevant credentials.
**Performance:** Strong when the claim is genuinely surprising and backed by science. Weak when it feels clickbaity without payoff.

## Category 4: Curiosity Gap

**Pattern:** Hints at information without revealing it. Creates an information gap the viewer must close.
**Why it works:** Zeigarnik effect: open loops in the brain demand closure. The viewer cannot scroll past an unanswered question that feels relevant to them.
**Performance:** Consistent mid-tier. Best for recipe reveals, result posts, and list-format content.

## Compound Hooks (Two or More Techniques Combined)

Compound hooks consistently outperform single-category hooks. The scoring system awards 5 bonus points for compound hooks (see scoring-system.md, Hook Strength category).

### Compound Formulas

| Combination | Formula | Example | Expected Performance |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Correction + Identity | "Die meisten [Zielgruppe] [Aktion] voellig falsch." | "Die meisten Frauen ueber 40 trainieren ihren Bauch voellig falsch." | Top tier. Combines the #1 hook type with personal targeting. |
| Correction + Bold Claim | "[Correction]. [Ueberraschende Wahrheit]." | "Dein Po wird nicht straffer, weil du MEHR machst. Weniger ist der Schluessel." | Top tier. Double pattern interrupt. |
| Bold Claim + Curiosity Gap | "[Provokante Aussage]. [Unaufgeloeste Frage]." | "Cardio macht dich nicht schlank. Was dann? Ich zeig's dir in 60 Sekunden." | High. Statement hooks, question retains. |
| Relatable Pain + Correction | "[Frustration]? Dann machst du [das hier] falsch." | "Du nimmst trotz Diaet nicht ab? Dann machst du das hier falsch." | High. Empathy creates trust, correction creates curiosity. |
| Identity + Curiosity Gap | "Wenn du [Situation], musst du [hidden info] wissen." | "Wenn du ueber 40 bist, musst du das ueber deinen Stoffwechsel wissen." | Mid-high. Targets audience, then withholds the answer. |
| Bold Claim + Identity | "[Provokante Aussage]. Besonders nach [Alter/Phase]." | "Du brauchst keine Diaet. Du brauchst einen anderen Rhythmus. Besonders nach 40." | High. Universal claim, then personal filter. |
| Correction + Curiosity Gap | "[Du machst das falsch]. Und die Loesung ist [hint]." | "Du trainierst deinen Ruecken falsch. Und die Loesung hat nichts mit Gewichten zu tun." | High. Correction creates stop, gap creates retention. |
| Relatable Pain + Identity | "[Frustration], oder? [Zielgruppe], aufgepasst." | "Abends alles essen und sich dafuer hassen? Frauen ueber 40, das ist hormonell. Nicht deine Schuld." | Mid-high. Empathy + age validation. |
| Identity + Bold Claim + Correction | Triple compound. Reserve for flagship content. | "Wenn du ueber 50 bist, vergiss Cardio. Die meisten Frauen in deinem Alter trainieren voellig falsch." | Top tier. Use sparingly (max 1x/week). |

### Compound Hook Construction Rules

1. **Lead with the strongest element.** Correction or Bold Claim in the first clause. Identity or Curiosity Gap in the second.
2. **Maximum 2 elements per spoken hook.** Triple compounds work only in text overlays where the viewer can parse all three at their own speed.
3. **Each element must be complete.** Do not fragment a hook category. "Die meisten Frauen..." without the correction is just a sentence fragment, not a Correction hook.
4. **Test the standalone:** Cover the second element. Does the first still stop the scroll? If not, reorder.

## Hook Scoring Rubric

Tied directly to the Hook Strength category in scoring-system.md (25 points total, 25% weight):

| Sub-Criterion | Points | What Qualifies |
|--------------|--------|----------------|
| Hook type identified | 5 | Uses a documented category from this library (Correction, Identity, Bold Claim, Curiosity Gap, Relatable Pain) |
| Curiosity gap present | 5 | First 3 seconds create information asymmetry. Viewer has a question they cannot answer without watching. |
| Pattern interrupt | 5 | Breaks scrolling behavior through visual surprise, unexpected statement, or direct address. Viewer's thumb stops. |
| First 3 seconds explicit | 5 | The exact opening words and visual are specified in the content brief. No vagueness like "start with something interesting." |
| Compound hook used | 5 | Two or more hook categories combined in a single opening. Must be intentional and documented. |

**Scoring bands:**
- 0-5: No clear hook, generic opening ("Hey, in diesem Video...")
- 6-10: Basic hook present but weak execution or wrong category for topic
- 11-15: Single strong hook mechanic, well-executed
- 16-20: Strong hook with clear category identification, matches topic well
- 21-25: Compound hook with proven formula adapted for originality

## A/B Testing Methodology

How to test two hooks for the same content:

### Same-Content, Different-Hook Test

1. **Produce the same Reel twice** with identical content body (seconds 4-60) but different opening hooks (seconds 0-3).
2. **Post Version A on Tuesday** (peak engagement day). Post Version B the following Tuesday.
3. **Compare after 7 days:** Save rate, completion rate, and reach. Likes alone are not a valid comparison metric.
4. **Control for:** Day of week (same day), time of day (same slot), and content body (identical).

### What to Test

| Test Variable | Version A | Version B | What You Learn |
|--------------|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Hook category | Correction hook | Curiosity Gap hook | Which category drives more saves for this topic |
| Hook length | 5-word hook | 12-word hook | Whether brevity or specificity wins for this topic |
| Direct vs. indirect | "Du machst das falsch" | "Die meisten Frauen machen das falsch" | Whether direct address outperforms general address |
| Visual hook type | Text overlay only | Talking head + text | Whether face presence affects 3-second retention |
| Specificity | "Training voellig falsch" | "Kniebeuge voellig falsch" | Whether specific or general framing drives more curiosity |

### Minimum Test Criteria

- At least 24 hours between posting variants (no same-day tests)
- Same content category (do not compare a Recipe hook vs. a Workout hook)
- At least 3 test cycles before drawing conclusions from a pattern
- Record results in a simple table: Date, Hook A, Hook B, Reach A, Reach B, Save Rate A, Save Rate B, Winner

## Hook Adaptation Rules

Every hook from this library must be adapted before use. The 2026 Instagram algorithm penalizes recycled formats (see algorithm-2026.md, Originality Score section). Templates are starting points, not copy-paste solutions.

### Adaptation Process

1. **Select the template** from the appropriate category for your topic.
2. **Fill in the variables** with topic-specific language.
3. **Apply one of these originality transforms:**

| Transform | Description | Example (from template #1) |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------------|
| Specificity upgrade | Replace generic terms with highly specific ones | "Bauchmuskeln" becomes "unteren Bauchmuskeln ab 45" |
| Authority stamp | Add creator's credentials or experience | "Als [creator's qualification] mit [X] Jahren Erfahrung: ..." |
| Recency anchor | Tie to current research or trend | "Eine neue Studie zeigt: Die meisten Frauen trainieren..." |
| Emotional layer | Add feeling-words to the factual hook | "Ich sehe das taeglich und es tut mir weh: Die meisten Frauen..." |
| Contrast frame | Add what the correct approach is (in hint form) | "Die meisten Frauen trainieren ihren Bauch falsch. Es sind nicht die Crunches." |

4. **Check originality:** Has this exact phrasing been used in the last 90 days? If yes, apply another transform.
5. **Check the 10-word rule:** Can the on-screen text version fit in 10 words or fewer?

### Red Flags (Hook Needs More Adaptation)

- You can Google the exact phrase and find 5+ other creators using it
- The hook could apply to any creator in the niche, not specifically to the creator's unique angle (defined in account-baseline.md)
- No specific detail that makes the viewer think "this is about MY situation"
- The hook has been used on this account within the last 60 days

## Platform-Specific Hook Adjustments

The same content often appears in different formats. The hook must be adjusted for each platform context.

### Reel Hook (Video, 9:16, first 3 seconds)

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Spoken words | Max 15-20 words in first 3 seconds. Slower speech than normal, emphasis on key words. |
| Text overlay | Max 10 words on screen. Large font, high contrast. Must be readable without sound. |
| Visual | Creator's face visible, direct eye contact with camera. Body language: confident, slightly leaning forward. |
| Audio | No silence in first 3 seconds. Start speaking immediately. Music bed underneath, not competing with voice. |
| Pattern interrupt | First frame should look different from the content body. Change in framing, background color, or text style. |

### Carousel Cover Hook (Static image, 4:5)

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Text | Max 8-10 words. The hook IS the cover. No intro slides like "Swipe fuer 5 Tipps." |
| Font | Large, bold, high-contrast. Same font as brand guidelines. |
| Visual | Creator's face or relevant visual visible. Text does NOT cover the face. |
| Design | The cover must work as a standalone post. If someone never swipes, they should still get the hook. |
| Slide 2 | Also functions as a hook (algorithm shows carousel again starting from slide 2). Mirror the hook with a different angle. |

### Story Opener Hook (Vertical, first slide)

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Text | Max 15 words per slide. Centered, large font. |
| Duration | 5-7 seconds for text slides. Enough time to read but short enough to feel urgent. |
| Interactive | Consider adding a poll or question sticker directly on the hook slide ("Machst du das auch?") |
| Sequence | Hook slide, then 2-4 value slides, then CTA slide. Hook must make them tap through all slides. |

## Visual Hook Components

What the viewer SEES in the first frame matters as much as what they hear. These elements stop the thumb before the audio hook lands.

### First-Frame Visual Checklist

| Component | What Works | What Fails |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Creator's expression | Surprised, serious, or "I need to tell you something" face | Smiling, neutral, or posed |
| Eye contact | Direct camera eye contact | Looking away, looking at phone, looking at equipment |
| Body language | Leaning slightly toward camera, one hand gesturing | Arms crossed, standing passively, back to camera |
| Background | Clean, recognizable (creator's typical setting) | Cluttered, dark, unfamiliar |
| Text overlay position | Upper third, within safe zone (Y:150-1570px) | Bottom (covered by UI), too small, outside safe zone |
| Text overlay style | Bold, high contrast (white on dark or dark on light card) | Thin font, low contrast, decorative fonts |
| Color | Brand colors or high-contrast outfit against background | Outfit blends into background, everything same tone |
| Props/context clue | Holding relevant item (dumbbell, food, timer) only when it adds to the hook | Random props, nothing in hands when topic is exercise |

### First-Frame Pattern Templates

1. **The "Listen up" frame:** Creator facing camera, one hand raised (index finger), text overlay with the correction/claim. Best for authority-driven correction content.
2. **The "Watch this" frame:** Creator mid-action (e.g., demonstrating incorrect vs. correct approach), freeze frame with text overlay. Best for how-to correction posts.
3. **The "Before I start" frame:** Close-up of creator's face, serious expression, text overlay with the hook. Best for talking-head education posts.
4. **The "Result reveal" frame:** Split-screen or side-by-side showing transformation or comparison. Best for before/after content.

When a topic in the creator's niche starts trending:

4. **Never chase trends outside the niche.** Only react to topics within the creator's defined expertise (see account-baseline.md).

## Hook Length Rules

### Spoken Hook (Audio)

| Metric | Rule | Why |
|--------|------|-----|
| Max words | 15-20 words | More than 20 words in 3 seconds requires rushed speech that kills clarity |
| Max duration | 3 seconds | Algorithm evaluates 3-second retention as the primary distribution gate |
| Pacing | Slightly slower than conversational speed | Key words need emphasis. Rushing signals nervousness, not authority. |
| First word | Must be a strong word (not "Also", "Ja", "Hey") | First word determines whether the audio hook registers |

### On-Screen Text Hook

| Metric | Rule | Why |
|--------|------|-----|
| Max words | 10 words | Must be fully readable in under 1 second while scrolling |
| Max lines | 2 lines | More than 2 lines becomes a paragraph, not a hook |
| Font size | Minimum 60pt equivalent on 1080px wide frame | Smaller text is unreadable on mobile in-feed |
| Position | Upper third, within safe zone | Bottom is covered by UI elements |

## Hook Graveyard: What Does NOT Work

These hook patterns consistently underperform across most niches. Verify against the target audience (defined in account-baseline.md) before using any of them.

### Dead Hooks

| Hook Pattern | Why It Fails | What to Use Instead |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| "Hey ihr Lieben, heute zeige ich euch..." | Zero curiosity, zero pattern interrupt. The viewer has already scrolled past. | Jump straight into the Correction or Bold Claim. No greeting. |
| "5 Tipps fuer einen flachen Bauch" | Generic listicle framing. Every fitness account posts this. Algorithm flags as low-originality. | "Die meisten Frauen trainieren ihren Bauch voellig falsch." (Correction) |
| "POV: Du bist im Gym und..." | (fitness niche example) Overused TikTok format. 2026 algorithm penalizes recycled trends. Mismatches with audiences that expect expertise over entertainment. | Direct talking-head with text overlay. Authority-driven audiences want expertise, not memes. |
| "Was ich in einem Tag esse" (without twist) | (fitness niche example) Overdone format. Only works with a genuine twist that adds identity or specificity. | Add Identity Trigger + specific result to the format. |
| "Motivation Monday!" / generic motivational | Audience wants actionable advice, not inspiration posters. Low save rate, low DM-sends. | Replace with Correction or Bold Claim that teaches something. |
| Fear-based without solution | "Wenn du das machst, zerstoerst du deinen Stoffwechsel!" without offering the correct approach. Creates anxiety, not value. | Always pair the warning with the solution: "...und hier ist, was du stattdessen tun solltest." |
| "Schockierende Wahrheit ueber..." | Clickbait phrasing. Triggers skepticism in mature audiences. They have seen too many clickbait headlines. | Use Curiosity Gap with specificity: (fitness niche example) "Was Wissenschaftler 2026 ueber Fasten herausgefunden haben." |
| "Nur 1% wissen das..." | Overused growth-hack hook. Signals "I learned this from a growth course." Not authentic to any creator's genuine voice. | "Das sagt dir kein [relevant authority]." or "Ich muss dir etwas sagen, das kaum jemand zugeben will." |
| "Glow-up Transformation" | Visual-first format with no educational value. Wrong audience for expertise-driven accounts. | Transformation content yes, but framed as story + education, not visual montage. |
| "Ich hab's getestet, damit du es nicht musst" | Overused review format. Works for product reviews, not for educational content. | "Als [creator's qualification] sage ich dir: [correction based on testing/experience]." |
| "Nummer 4 wird dich ueberraschen" | Buzzfeed-era clickbait. Audience recognizes and resents it. | If using numbered lists, put the strongest item first, not as a tease. |
| "Storytime!" | Too casual for educational fitness content. Signals unstructured rambling. | If telling a story, use Relatable Pain format with specific details in the first sentence. |

### Why These Fail: The Common Thread

Every dead hook shares one or more of these traits:
1. **No specificity.** Could apply to any account, any audience, any topic.
2. **No curiosity gap.** The viewer can predict what comes next.
3. **Wrong audience register.** Written for a different demographic than the target audience.
4. **No authority signal.** Does not leverage the creator's credentials, experience, or unique perspective.
5. **Overused format.** The 2026 algorithm specifically penalizes recycled hook patterns.

## Hook Writing Rules (Non-Negotiable)

### Content Rules

- Write the hook as the exact first-3-second script, not just the concept
- No filler openings ("Hey!", "Willkommen", "In diesem Video...", "Also...")
- Hooks must stand alone. If the hook only makes sense with context, rewrite it.
- Test: Would this hook make someone stop scrolling even without sound? (text overlay test)
- Every hook must pass the "Would I stop scrolling for this?" test from the perspective of the target audience (defined in account-baseline.md)

### Formatting Rules

- No em dashes in any hook. Use comma, colon, or period instead. (See content-rules.md, Forbidden Patterns)
- No exclamation marks in the first sentence (reads as spam)
- Capitalize single emphasis words: "Du machst das KOMPLETT falsch" not "DU MACHST DAS KOMPLETT FALSCH"
- German only. No English words unless they are established German vocabulary (e.g., "Workout", "Fitness", "Cardio")

### Selection Logic

Use this decision tree to pick the right hook category for any topic:

```
Does the topic involve a common misconception?
  YES -> CORRECTION (default first choice, #1 performer)
    Can you target a specific audience segment?
      YES -> CORRECTION + IDENTITY (compound, top tier)
      NO  -> CORRECTION standalone

  NO -> Does the content target a specific sub-segment (age, life phase, habit)?
    YES -> IDENTITY TRIGGER
      Can you add a surprising claim?
        YES -> IDENTITY + BOLD CLAIM (compound)
        NO  -> IDENTITY standalone

    NO -> Does the content contradict a common belief?
      YES -> BOLD CLAIM
        Can you withhold the answer?
          YES -> BOLD CLAIM + CURIOSITY GAP (compound)
          NO  -> BOLD CLAIM standalone

      NO -> Does the content reveal a specific number, result, or finding?
        YES -> CURIOSITY GAP

        NO -> Is the content emotional, transformational, or personal?
          YES -> RELATABLE PAIN
          NO  -> Default to CORRECTION (reframe the topic as a correction)
```

**Rule of thumb:** When in doubt, use CORRECTION. It is a consistently top-performing hook type across niches. You can almost always reframe a topic as "most [target audience] do X wrong."

## Usage Protocol

When creating any Instagram content:

1. **Identify the content topic and category** (Correction, Workout, Recipe, Age-Defiance, Personal).
2. **Use the selection logic decision tree** to pick the hook category.
3. **Select 2-3 candidate templates** from the relevant category tables above.
4. **Apply the adaptation rules** to make each template original (specificity upgrade, authority stamp, recency anchor, emotional layer, or contrast frame).
5. **Build a compound hook** if possible (combine 2 categories).
6. **Check against the Hook Graveyard** to ensure no dead patterns are being used.
7. **Verify hook length:** max 10 words on screen, max 15-20 words spoken, max 3 seconds.
8. **Score the hook** using the Hook Scoring Rubric (target: 20+/25 for flagship content).
9. **Adjust for platform:** Reel hook, Carousel cover, or Story opener rules.
10. **Document the hook in the content brief** with exact wording, text overlay, and visual description.
